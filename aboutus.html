About Our Wildlife Sanctuary

Our Story

Founded in 2025, the Wild Nature Wildlife Sanctuary began as a small conservation effort to protect local species affected by habitat loss. What started as a 50-acre refuge has now grown into a 500-acre haven for over 200 species of animals, many of which are endangered or threatened.

Our sanctuary is dedicated to the rescue, rehabilitation, and protection of wildlife, with a special focus on species native to East Africa. We work closely with conservationists, researchers, and local communities to create sustainable solutions for wildlife preservation.

Our Mission & Values

Mission: To protect and conserve wildlife through rescue, rehabilitation, education, and habitat preservation while promoting harmonious coexistence between humans and animals.

Core Values:

  • Compassion: We treat all living creatures with respect and dignity.
  • Conservation: We are committed to preserving biodiversity for future generations.
  • Education: We believe knowledge is key to changing attitudes about wildlife.
  • Community: We work with local populations to create sustainable solutions.
  • Innovation: We employ cutting-edge techniques in animal care and habitat management.

Our Conservation Work

At Green Valley Wildlife Sanctuary, we operate several key conservation programs:

  • Wildlife Rescue and Rehabilitation Center
  • Breeding Programs for Endangered Species
  • Anti-Poaching Initiatives
  • Habitat Restoration Projects
  • Community Education Programs

Our team of veterinarians, biologists, and conservationists work tirelessly to ensure the animals in our care receive the best possible treatment and have the opportunity to return to the wild whenever possible.
